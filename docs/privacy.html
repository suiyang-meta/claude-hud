HUD for Claude — Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 16, 2026

HUD for Claude is a two-part product: a Chrome browser extension and a macOS desktop app. Both are built by a single independent developer. This policy explains exactly what data is read, where it goes, and what is stored.

Short version

Your data never leaves your computer. There are no servers, no analytics, no accounts, no tracking. The extension reads four public usage numbers from claude.ai/settings/usage and sends them to the desktop app running on your own machine via localhost. That's it.

What the extension reads

  • The four usage percentages shown on claude.ai/settings/usage: current session usage, weekly all-models usage, weekly Sonnet-only usage, and daily routine runs.
  • The reset timestamps shown on that same page.

The extension does not read:

  • Any of your conversations or prompts.
  • Your account information, email, or name.
  • Any other page on claude.ai besides the usage page.
  • Any other website.

Where the data goes

The scraped numbers are sent to ws://localhost:27843 — a WebSocket server running on your own computer, started by the HUD for Claude desktop app. Nothing is sent over the internet. Nothing is sent to the developer or any third party.

What is stored

  • In the browser: The latest usage numbers are cached in chrome.storage.local so the extension popup can display them instantly when clicked. This cache is overwritten every 30 seconds and never leaves your browser profile.
  • On your Mac: The desktop app stores a tiny preferences file (your "Launch at Login" choice) in the macOS application support directory. No usage data is persisted to disk.

Third parties

None. No analytics SDKs, no crash reporters, no ad networks, no remote APIs.

Children

HUD for Claude is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect any data from them.

Changes to this policy

If the product ever starts collecting different data, this policy will be updated and the "Last updated" date at the top of this page will change. Material changes will also be announced on the product page.
